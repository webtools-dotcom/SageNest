How this estimate works

For singleton pregnancies, guidance is based on the Institute of Medicine (IOM) BMI categories. In the first trimester (weeks 1–13), gain is usually modest. After that, expected gain is often tracked as an average weekly rate through the second and third trimesters.

Real pregnancies vary. Your provider may personalize targets based on symptoms, fetal growth, metabolic health, or multiple gestation factors.

FAQ

How accurate is this pregnancy weight gain calculator?

This calculator gives an educational estimate using BMI-based guidance and should be personalized with your clinician.

Does this calculator replace medical advice?

No. It does not replace prenatal care, diagnosis, or treatment from a licensed healthcare professional.

What if I am carrying twins?

Twin pregnancies usually have higher total gain targets, and your care team should provide individualized goals.

Should I try to lose weight during pregnancy?

Do not intentionally restrict or lose weight during pregnancy unless your clinician gives a specific plan.
